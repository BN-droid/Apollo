Diet: Seniors should follow a balanced diet that includes fruits and vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins (chicken, fish, legumes), and healthy fats. It's important to maintain an adequate intake of fiber, calcium, and vitamin D to support bone health and digestion.

Physical Activity: Seniors should aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week, plus muscle-strengthening and balance exercises twice a week. Recommended activities include walking, swimming, tai chi, gentle yoga, light resistance exercises, and stretching.

Gym: Seniors can benefit from using light machines and weights at the gym, as well as participating in gentle exercise classes, yoga, or pilates suitable for seniors. It's important to ensure activities are safe and appropriate for their physical abilities.

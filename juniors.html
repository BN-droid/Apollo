Diet: Children should have a balanced diet that includes plenty of fresh fruits and vegetables, lean proteins (such as chicken, fish, eggs, and legumes), whole grains, and dairy products. It's important to limit the consumption of foods high in sugars and saturated fats, such as packaged snacks and sugary drinks.

Physical Activity: Children should engage in at least 60 minutes of moderate-to-vigorous physical activity each day. Recommended activities include outdoor play like running and jumping, cycling, swimming, gymnastics classes, and team sports like soccer, basketball, and volleyball.

Gym: For young children, the gym is not necessary. Focus on playful activities and active games that promote motor skills development and socialization.
